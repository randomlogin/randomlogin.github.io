Academic and education

For me education is in some sense broadening the horizons, so my studies vary from linguistics and cryptography to quantum physics and cosmology. Still, I’d introduce myself as a mathematician.

Graduated from Belarusian State University with bachelor’s degree thesis: the density of Cartan subalgebras in the variety of abelian subalgebras of complex semisimple Lie algebras. Then I’ve spent a year on master’s programme in mathematics at HSE faculty of mathematics with term paper topic: structure of the automorphism group of a finite-dimensional associative algebra.

Next, I proceeded education on Master’s in Mathematical Logic and Foundations of Computer Science in University of Paris which hopefully will shed light on few bothering questions about the so-called concept of information. <!–

My mathematical background starts with a solid foundation in topology, algebra and analysis, proceeding with further knowledge of differential geometry, algebraic geometry,
Lie theory
, category theory, computer science, model theory, classical physics as well as

and computational mathematics. I hate advanced branches of analysis, though.

Sometimes I participate algebra-related seminars of Moscow Mathematical Society, Ivan Arjantsev’s (he’s the scientific advisor of mine) seminar on the additive group actions, and participated in dessin d’enfant seminar of George Shabat in the past. I’ve also took few courses at Independent University of Moscow.

After

Business

Long time ago I was a freelancer, doing some web development, maintaining cryptographic gem, creating proof-of-concept solutions (for example in haskell), and even
writing educational articles about cryptography.

As a professional mathematician with knowledge of all the intrinsic capabilities and pitfalls of the modern cryptography, it was quite straightforward to spend few years in the newly emergent blockchain sphere.

From the most recent activities, I was a
CEO of an ICO
startup (though, I consider it a fail), being
the jack of all trades:
writing the whitepapers, creating smart contracts, pitching the project at conferences (d10e conference in Seoul), organising the workflow, hiring and firing employees, dealing with clients, developing kyc policies, even node and server management. Still, I left in the mid 2018.

During that time I was also a technical consultant/architect in a research/working group for another blockchain project developing the ledger based on the DPoS consensus algorithm, with Turing-complete smart contracts backward compatible with Ethereum contracts, sophisticated services like built-in name service, state machine proxy, broad and delegateable account rights, and innovative funding approach. My participation included cryptography consulting, virtual machine workflow (backward compatibility with the Ethereum smart-contracts), key management, economics and security from the game theory perspective. The project was backed by venture capital fund.

I really enjoyed creating smart contracts, as they are tiny, very efficient and independent pieces of code demanding no library dependencies. Here are some examples:

  1. Initial Development Offering concept, description in the source code.
  2. Typical token and crowdsale contracts for tokens to be purchased for Ether. The price is fixed in USD, so it’s updated via server script which pulled info from the Bitfinex exchange.

In 2019 I worked as a blockchain developer for dexpa.io, porting Ethereum smart-contracts to the TON blockchain and communicating with TON labs to help them test and debug SDKs they are creating.

Later, 2019-2020 security anaylist at FutureComes, making security audits for web and crypto projects (wallets, exchanges and exchange services).

For now (summer 2020) I’m involved in Handshake project, but I’m open for interesting proposals.
